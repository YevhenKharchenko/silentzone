Stillroom

Experience

Stillroom is not about winning or finishing.
It is about being present.

Rooms subtly react to:
How long you stay
Where you look
How often you interact
Periods of complete stillness

The experience unfolds differently depending on how much attention you give — or withhold.

Environment Design

The rooms feel personal, maybe a bit unsettling, but they're not trying to scare you.

  • Minimalist rooms with shifting layouts

  • Make thoughtful, logic-driven decisions — timing matters more than speed

  • Plan ahead to avoid blocking future options

  • Progress through objectives and unlock new mechanics as the game evolves

Interaction System

Weird thing is, when you stop trying so hard, the rooms start showing you things.

Slow movement between rooms
Gentle object focus instead of direct actions
Pauses trigger environmental changes
Silence is treated as an input

Atmosphere & Tone

Visuals are desaturated, sound design is minimal, and nothing competes for attention.

The emotional tone is:
Calm but introspective
Slightly melancholic
Safe, yet emotionally open
